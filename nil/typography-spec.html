Section 1

Foundations

The system stands on two type families and a small set of ink colors. Everything else — every heading, label, and caption — is built by varying the size, weight, and case of these two families against the ink palette. Resist adding a third family or new colors; reach for the existing tokens first.

Serif — long-form, display

Source Serif 4
Italic and optical sizes:
Aa Aa · Aa Aa Aa
Weights used: 300, 400, 500, 600

Use the serif for the cover title, all section & subsection headings, chart titles, the lead paragraph, footnote markers, and chart source lines. Always honor the optical-size axis: the cover title sits at opsz 60, section heads at opsz 36, body and captions at opsz 14–18.

Sans — UI, labels, body

Inter
Weights used: 400, 500, 600, 700
Aa Aa Aa Aa

Use Inter for body copy, all chart labels & ticks, annotations, table headers and cells, TOC sub-entries, page chrome (running head, folio, footnotes), eyebrows, figure labels, and any small uppercase label. Always pair tabular numerics (font-variant-numeric: tabular-nums) on numeric columns.

Ink & accent palette

Text is layered: black for emphasis (--ink), near-black for body (--ink-2), warm grey for secondary/captions (--ink-3), and a paler grey for hairlines & markers (--ink-4). The accent blue is reserved for eyebrows, figure labels, brand marks, and the cover date.

  • --ink#1A1714Cover title, section & subsection heads, table emphasis, strong
  • --ink-2#2C2823Body copy default, axis text, table cells
  • --ink-3#6B645ACaptions, eyebrow on cover, page chrome, sub-sub TOC
  • --ink-4#9A9389Hairline rules, trendlines, secondary markers
  • --accent#015C9CCover date, eyebrows, figure labels, primary chart series
  • cover bg#F3F2EACover page paper color only — do not use on content pages

Chart palette (categorical)

  • c-1#015C9CPrimary — single-series default
  • c-2#C77A20Contrast / lead-finding amber
  • c-3#CEC96BThird series
  • c-4#51B196Fourth series
  • c-5#A8352CFifth series
  • c-6#918BEDSixth series

Muted (de-emphasis encoding)

  • c-muted#7E8A99Background data — paints the “everyone else” series when one is highlighted

Use #7E8A99 to mute non-focus data points so a single highlighted series stands out. Typical pattern: paint every line / dot in --c-muted, then re-paint the focus series on top in --c-2 (or another accent). The muted layer carries the general trend; the highlight carries the story.

Rules for using color

  1. Color encodes meaning, not decoration. Every color in a chart must carry a specific function — a category, a series, a threshold, a highlight. If a color is not earning its keep, remove it.
  2. Keep most charts to two to four colors. A reader can hold only a few categories in working memory at once. When a chart truly needs more series, encode the rest with the muted color and let the focus series carry the story.
  3. Use color to highlight; mute the rest. Paint the focus series in an accent (typically --c-1 or --c-2) and the supporting data in --c-muted (#7E8A99). The muted layer carries the trend; the highlight carries the finding.

Section 10

Decision rules

The system is small on purpose. Before introducing a new style, work through this list.

  1. Serif or sans? Serif for the cover, all headings, the lead, the colophon, chart titles, and chart sources. Sans for everything else.
  2. Body size is 12 px. Do not push body text below 12 px. Footnotes (9 px) are the only exception, and they live next to the folio.
  3. No trailing periods on headings. Section headings, subsection headings, and TOC entries end without a period. Chart titles end with a period (they read as a finding).
  4. No all-caps body or headings. Uppercase is reserved for eyebrows, figure labels, running heads, and similar 9–11 px chrome.
  5. Color is for emphasis, not decoration. Default ink layers: --ink (titles, strong), --ink-2 (body), --ink-3 (captions, chrome), --ink-4 (hairlines). Reach for --accent only for eyebrows, figure labels, the cover date, and the primary chart series.
  6. Tabular numerals in tables and charts. Always set font-variant-numeric: tabular-nums on numeric cells, axis ticks, page numbers, and TOC page numbers.
  7. One lead per section. A p.lead never appears twice on the same page.
  8. Cover meta is two lines. Series name in ink-3 weight 600; date in accent weight 700. Never combine them on one line.
  9. Subtitle is optional, source is required. Every chart needs a .chart-source. The .chart-subtitle can be omitted when redundant.
  10. No monospace. Numerals use Inter with tabular figures. Avoid mono fonts in body, charts, and tables.
